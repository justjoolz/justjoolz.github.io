Music Story Process Systems

A few working notes · not a manifesto

Follow,
don’t force.

I do not begin with a finished idea and impose it on silence. I listen for what is already moving.

The process is less mysterious than it sounds and more mysterious than a checklist allows.

Listening for what arises.

First I listen to what is present. Then I listen for what wants to enter. I follow that movement until the music starts revealing its own rules. Long before I had language such as wisdom mind or contemplative practice, the sounds led and I followed.

01 · ~ness

Recognised,
not defined.

Nik Di Benedetto and I used ~ness for an elusive rightness in people, sounds and situations. It cannot be reduced to technique, genre, status or taste. Explaining it too thoroughly tends to make it leave.

02 · Negative space

The gap is active.

Space around a sound is not waiting to be filled. It is where another rhythm can enter, tension can gather and the listener can complete what the music leaves unsaid.

03 · Repetition

Again is never the same.

A repeated phrase changes because the context changes—and because the listener does. Dub, dance music, mantra and meditation each know something about staying long enough for difference to appear.

04 · Machines

Instrument,
not identity.

A groovebox from 1996 and a modern software system can both become instruments. Their value is not novelty. It is whether they let a hand, ear or intuition reach the structure while it is alive.
